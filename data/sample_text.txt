The Dawn of Space Dreams: From Myths to Missiles
Humans have gazed skyward forever. In India, Rigveda hymns from 1500 BCE described Surya (Sun) as a golden chariot and Chandra (Moon) as a cosmic wanderer—early astrology roots tying stars to fate.

Fast-forward to 1957: Sputnik 1 beeps from orbit, igniting the Space Race. USSR's fist-sized sphere terrified the West. USA countered with Explorer 1 (1958), discovering Van Allen radiation belts.

Key milestones:

1961: Yuri Gagarin orbits Earth—"Khalida!" (Off we go!).

1969: Apollo 11. Neil Armstrong's "one small step" on lunar dust. Buzz Aldrin's bootprint? Still there, vacuum-preserved.

1981: Space Shuttle Columbia launches reusable era.

2000s: ISS assembly, a floating lab orbiting at 28,000 km/h.

India shines too. ISRO's 1975 Mahabharata satellite kicked off; 2014's Mangalyaan Mars orbiter cost less than Hollywood's Gravity ($74M vs. $100M). PSLV rockets now haul payloads like clockwork.

Space flipped tech: GPS from satellites, memory foam from NASA cushions, even your Python IDE traces to Apollo guidance code.

Cosmic Neighborhood: Our Solar System Tour
Our Sun's family spans 5.2 billion km to Neptune. Let's zoom.

Inner Rocks: Mercury to Mars
Mercury: Scorched hellscape, 430°C days, -180°C nights. Messenger probe (2015) mapped ice in craters—water in hell?

Venus: Runaway greenhouse, 460°C, sulfuric rain. Soviet Venera landers survived 2 hours; NASA's DAVINCI+ (2029) dives atmosphere.

Earth: Goldilocks zone star. 71% ocean, magnetic shield vs. solar wind.

Mars: Rusty red, Olympus Mons (22km volcano), Valles Marineris (longer than USA). Perseverance rover (2021) hunts biosignatures; Ingenuity helicopter flew 72 times.

Gas Giants: Jupiter and Saturn
Jupiter's Great Red Spot? Storm bigger than Earth, raging 400+ years. Juno probe peers into metallic hydrogen depths. Europa's subsurface ocean? 2x Earth's water, possible life. NASA's Europa Clipper launches 2024.

Saturn's rings: Ice/rock debris, 1cm to house-sized. Cassini (2004-2017) plunged through gaps, revealing Enceladus geysers spewing organics.

Outer Realms: Uranus, Neptune, Pluto
Ice giants tilt wildly—Uranus sideways, maybe hit by Earth-sized protoplanet. Voyager 2 (1986) flybys remain sole visits. Pluto (dwarf planet) hearts New Horizons snapped (2015).

Oort Cloud? Hypothetical comet shell, 100,000 AU out—2 light-years!

Black Holes and Beyond: Galactic Wonders
Space scales up insanely. Milky Way: 100,000 light-years wide, 100-400 billion stars. Supermassive black hole Sagittarius A* (4M solar masses) imaged 2022 by Event Horizon Telescope.
